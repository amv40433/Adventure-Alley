Mr. Revera

The apparent culprit should’ve been Mr. Revera — Valerie’s arch nemesis.
He’s won second place every year since Valerie started baking, and some men can only swallow so much defeat before they choke.
But something about it felt too neat.
Too clean.

Revera didn’t strike me as the type to steal a pie.
Break a window? Sure.
Slash a tire? Maybe.
But pie theft?
That takes a different breed of desperation.
So I took a walk down to his shop — “Revera’s Finest Pastries,” a name that always tasted bitter in Valerie’s mouth.
The lights were off.
The blinds were drawn.
And the only thing greeting me at the door was the smell of burnt sugar and a “Closed Until Further Notice” sign that looked like it hadn’t moved in days.

That’s when I felt it — the shift.
The case didn’t get simpler.
It got deeper.
Someone wanted me to look at Revera.
Someone wanted this wrapped fast, slapped with a bow, and tossed in the “open and shut” drawer.
But the city doesn’t give out gifts without asking for something back.
And when I caught sight of the flour footprints leading away from Revera’s back door — footprints too small to belong to him, I knew I wasn’t just dealing with a stolen pie.
No.
This was something else.
Something with layers.
Like a good pie.
Or a bad lie.
Either way, I planned to cut right into it.
