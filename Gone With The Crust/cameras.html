Cameras

The security cameras weren’t glamorous, just dusty lenses and blinking lights tucked into corners like silent, judgmental witnesses.
I approached the monitor like a man approaching a confession, expecting nothing and hoping for everything.
The feed flickered, black-and-white ghosts moving in slow, jerky rhythm.
Frames captured the night: empty streets, closed shops, the contest booth in eerie silence.
And then,
There it was.
A shadow slipping past the booth.
Careful,
deliberate.
I leaned back in my chair, eyes still locked on the monitor, cigarette glowing in the dim light.
The pie might’ve been stolen, the tin empty, but the truth?
The truth never hides for long.
And tonight, it was finally showing itself.
