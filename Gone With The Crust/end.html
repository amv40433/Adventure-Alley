Who Done It?

The feed flickered, black-and-white ghosts moving in slow, jerky rhythm.
Frames captured the night: empty streets, closed shops, the contest booth in eerie silence.
And then…
the shadow moved.
Hands?
No.
Feet?
Not exactly.
It wasn’t a thief at all.
It was something smaller, faster, and far more unpredictable.
A dog!
One of the farmer’s, apparently loose last night, bounded through the booth.
Sniffed, circled, and then attacked the prize like a furry hurricane.
The pies didn’t stand a chance.
They were gone in seconds.
The crust, the filling, the glory, devoured, destroyed, and the tin licked clean like it had never held anything at all.

I leaned back in my chair, the cigarette burning down to nothing.
So this was the criminal mastermind: four paws, a wagging tail, and zero sense of fairness.
The city had thrown its weight into a drama, and the villain turned out to be… a dog.
I laughed, low, dry, and empty, because there was nothing else to do.
All the chasing, the canvassing, the careful note-taking, the flour trails, the shadows, the tension…
For a pie.
But that’s life in this city.
Sometimes the answers are too absurd to be believable.
Sometimes the thief isn’t clever, isn’t careful, or isn't human at all.
Valerie would be furious, sure.
And Revera… probably relieved.
But me?
I just shook my head.
The city never stops surprising you.
And neither do its dogs.
