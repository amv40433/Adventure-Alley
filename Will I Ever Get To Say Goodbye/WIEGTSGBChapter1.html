Will I Ever Get to Say Goodbye?

Chapter 1

I’ll never forget the first time I saw him. He was walking past me in the hallway of the apartment building I had just moved into. He was tall, broad-shouldered, with short curls the color of coal. Our eyes didn’t meet. He just walked out the doors as I stepped into the elevator, but somehow, that fleeting moment stayed with me.
Weeks passed, and I didn’t see him again. Eventually, I convinced myself to stop thinking about it, to stop wondering who he was. But then, one morning, I saw him, standing by the elevator doors, waiting. The same elevator I was about to take.
The elevator doors slid open with a soft chime. He glanced up when he saw me approach. For a second, I almost forgot how to move. Then I stepped inside, and the doors closed behind us with a low hum.
The air felt different in that small space—thicker somehow. I could hear my own heartbeat, feel the weight of the silence stretching between us. He smelled faintly of rain and laundry soap.
The elevator hummed softly as it began to move. I glanced at the glowing numbers overhead, pretending to care which floor we were on, even though I already knew.
The silence was unbearable. He stood beside me, tall and calm, his reflection flickering faintly against the brushed-metal walls.
I begin battling with myself, wondering if I should continue to pretend like I don't notice him or if I should say hello and introduce myself
