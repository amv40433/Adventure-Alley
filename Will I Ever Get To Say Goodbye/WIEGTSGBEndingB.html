Will I Ever Get to Say Goodbye?

As much as I liked him, I didn’t want him to think I had some ulterior motive. I genuinely just wanted to know him better. So I told myself I’d wait—wait until he said something, or until we crossed paths a few more times.
“Well, enjoy your run,” I said, forcing myself to turn toward my door.
“Enjoy your chicken,” he replied with a smile that lingered even after he jogged off.
After that afternoon, I ran into Elijah a few more times. Our conversations were short but pleasant. I never worked up the courage to ask him to hang out, and he never mentioned anything either.
Slowly, our encounters became less frequent.
Then, eventually, they stopped.
It was a Monday afternoon when I saw a moving truck outside the building and a young couple carrying boxes into the elevator.
I held the doors open for them as they loaded their belongings in.
“What floor?” I asked.
“Three,” the young man replied.
“Hey! That’s my floor,” I said brightly. “We must be neighbors. Welcome to the building!”
“Thanks!” the woman said. “I’m Jenna, and this is my husband, Miles.”
“Nice to meet you! I’m Adelyne—3B. What apartment are you two moving into?”
“Apartment 3F,” Miles replied.
The words hit harder than they should have.
Just like that… Elijah was gone.
No wonder I hadn’t seen him.
The elevator bell chimed, and the doors opened, interrupting my spiraling thoughts.
“Well, good luck with the move,” I said politely. “Hope to see you around.”
I didn’t mean that. Not really.
I never knew Elijah well, and I’m disappointed—maybe more than I should be—that I never got the chance to know him beyond those passing, neighborly moments. I never figured out what happened to him, or where he ended up, but I hope he’s doing well.
I still think about him sometimes—about that Sunday, about the dinner I didn’t invite him to, about the courage I didn’t have.
Maybe we could have been friends.
Maybe something more.
All I know is that he’s gone now, and I never got the chance to say goodbye.
